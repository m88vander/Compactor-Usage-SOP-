PURPOSE OF THIS SOP

To establish a safe, consistent, and thorough process for removing, emptying, cleaning, and reconnecting trash room dumpsters to the compactor/ram. Every step must be completed in order, and no trash room is returned to normal operation until the final inspection confirms the dumpster is properly secured and the chute and compactor are functioning correctly.

REQUIRED PPE

The following PPE must be worn whenever entering or working inside any trash room:

  • Safety gloves
  • Safety glasses

Inspect PPE before use and replace any damaged equipment. Do not enter a trash room without the required PPE.

PURPOSE OF THIS SOP

To establish a safe, consistent, and thorough process for removing, emptying, cleaning, and reconnecting trash room dumpsters to the compactor/ram. Every step must be completed in order, and no trash room is returned to normal operation until the final inspection confirms the dumpster is properly secured and the compactor is functioning correctly.

REQUIRED PPE

The following PPE must be worn whenever entering or working inside any trash room: safety gloves and safety glasses. Inspect PPE before use and replace any damaged equipment. Do not enter a trash room without the required PPE.

1 | Preparation

Put on the required PPE before entering the trash room, and identify the trash or recycling dumpster that will be removed.

Move the ram fully forward and stop it before disconnecting the dumpster. This keeps trash coming down the chute from spilling onto the floor while the dumpster is out for service.

2 | Removing the Dumpster

Release the dumpster from the ram/compactor. Close the dumpster side door and secure it tightly using the chains on both sides — this keeps the door from opening and contains debris while the dumpster is in transit. Carefully remove the dumpster from the trash room.

3 | Pre-Transport Safety Inspection

Before transporting the dumpster, verify the following:

  • The side door is fully closed and securely latched.
  • The caster brakes are engaged and functioning properly.
  • The wheels and casters show no damage or excessive wear.
  • The dumpster can be moved safely.
⚠️ If the wheels, casters, or brakes are damaged, do not use the dumpster tug/truck. Report the damage immediately to your supervisor and use a pallet jack to move the dumpster to the main compactor instead.
4 | Transport & Disposal

Carefully transport the dumpster to the main waste disposal area and empty its contents into the main compactor, following standard facility procedures.

5 | Cleaning & Returning the Dumpster

Wash the dumpster as needed to remove any remaining debris or residue, then return the clean dumpster to the trash room.

Before reconnecting the dumpster, clean any trash or debris from around the ram area and make sure the connection area is clear and safe.

🔧 Sensor check: clean the compactor/ram sensor and remove all debris from the sensor area. A dirty or obstructed sensor can cause the compactor to time out, so this step should not be skipped.

Reattach and securely connect the dumpster to the ram/compactor.

6 | Restoring Normal Operation

Reset the compactor from the control panel located in the trash room and verify that the system is operating normally.

Perform a final inspection to confirm:

  • The dumpster is properly connected to the ram.
  • The area around the ram is clean.
  • The trash room is clean, organized, and ready for normal operation.
⚠️ Safety Reminders
  • Always wear safety gloves and safety glasses while inside any trash room.
  • Always move the ram fully forward before disconnecting the dumpster.
  • Secure the dumpster side door before moving it out of the trash room.
  • Inspect the dumpster, wheels, casters, and brakes before every transport.
  • If there is any damage to the wheels, casters, or brakes, do not use the dumpster tug/truck — report it immediately and use a pallet jack if the dumpster needs to be moved.
  • Keep the compactor sensor area clear of debris to prevent timeouts.
  • Transport dumpsters carefully and stay aware of your surroundings to prevent injury or property damage.
  • Report any damaged dumpsters, wheels, brakes, ram, or compactor components before continuing the operation.
